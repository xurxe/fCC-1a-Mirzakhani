Honoring Maryam Mirzakhani

Iranian mathematical genius

May 12th 1977 - July 14th 2017

Maryam Mirzakhani was one of the most brilliant mathematicians of her generation. She dedicated most of her career to advanced geometry topics such as hyperbolic geometry, Teichmüller theory, symplectic geometry, and ergodic theory.

Mirzakhani's contributions revolutionized not only mathematics, but physics and geology as well. As a woman of color from a Muslim cultural background, she will forever remain a role model to people and demographics underrepresented in STEM fields.

1977

  • Mirzakhani is born in Tehran (Iran).

1988 - 1995

  • She attends Tehran Farzanegan School, a girls-only school administered by NODET (the National Organization for Development of Exceptional Talents).

1994

  • She and her schoolmate Roya Beheshti Zavareh are the first girls to compete at the Iranian National Olympiad. Mirzakhani wins the gold medal for mathematics.
  • She scores 41 out of 42 at the International Mathematical Olympiad in Hong Kong (China), being the first Iranian girl to win a gold medal.

1995

  • Mirzakhani wins her second gold medal for mathematics at the Iranian National Olympiad.
  • She scores 42 out of 42 at the International Mathematical Olympiad in Toronto (Canada), being the first Iranian to achieve a perfect score.

1999

  • She publishes Elementary Number Theory, Challenging Problems with Zavareh, fellow schoolmate and math Olympian.
  • She graduates with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from Sharif University of Technology (Iran).

2004

  • She earns a PhD in Mathematics from Harvard University (US).

2005

  • She is named one of the “Brilliant 10” by Popular Science.

2009

  • The American Society of Mathematics (ASM) honors her with the Blumenthal Award.
  • She becomes a professor of mathematics at Stanford University (US).

2013

  • She is awarded the ASM Ruth Lyttle Satter Prize in Mathematics.
  • The Simons Foundation (US) recognizes her with the Simons Investigator Award.
  • She is diagnosed with breast cancer.

2014

  • She receives the Clay Research Award from the Clay Mathematics Institute (UK).
  • During the International Congress of Mathematicians in Seoul (South Korea), she becomes the first woman and the first Iranian to receive the Fields Medal.
  • She is named one of Nature’s “10 people who mattered this year”.

2015

  • The French Academy of Sciences appoints her as a foreign associate.
  • She gets elected to the American Philosophical Society.

2016

  • She is the first Iranian woman to become a member of the National Academy of Sciences (US).

2017

  • She is selected into the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
  • She passes away from breast cancer at age 40.

2018

  • Thanks to the advocacy of the Women's Committee within the Iranian Mathematical Society, the International Council for Science declares Mirzakhani's birthday (May 12th) the International Women in Mathematics Day.
